🌿 About the Ganim Matching System

This system matches children to kindergartens in Ramat Gan using the Gale-Shapley stable matching algorithm. Parents submit preferences; the algorithm guarantees every child receives a placement and no family can "trade up" by switching — a property called stability.

📖 How Gale-Shapley Works

In each round, every unmatched parent proposes to their top remaining kindergarten. Each kindergarten tentatively holds its best applicants (up to capacity) and rejects the rest. Rejected parents move to their next choice. This repeats until everyone is placed.

Why "deferred" acceptance?

Kindergartens never commit permanently — they hold proposals tentatively and may swap if a better applicant arrives. This deferred approach is what guarantees stability.

What is a blocking pair?

A parent + kindergarten that both prefer each other over their current assignment. Gale-Shapley provably eliminates all blocking pairs — the result is always stable.

How our system ranks parents

Each parent receives a fit score based on distance, education type, religious orientation, price, tzaharon, and other preferences — weighted by their stated importance.

Distance blocking (our addition)

Before Gale-Shapley runs, each parent sees only kindergartens within their max distance. This cuts 65,000 theoretical pairs down to ~15,000 real candidates.
